Votely UI/UX Design

Goal


Increase voter turn-out in local elections.

The Problem



Through user research it was revealed that there is lower local election turnout due to:
  • Difficulty in understanding each positionâ€™s roles
  • Election info and candidate stances are unconsolidated, unclear
  • Government websites for registering and finding information are complicated
  • Low media coverage of elections

Plan



With this knowledge of the problem we wanted to create an app with:
  • information based on voter eligibility and location/region
  • help to fill out government forms in plain language
  • unbiased and comprehensive details of candidates and their stances
  • description of local elections and the scope of impact of each


Target Audience



  • Younger people around 20-30s
  • Politically Inactive People
  • New Voters

With all of this in mind, we created 3 personas likely to use the app, brainstormed scenarios, and developed lo-fi prototypes.

After exploring the different aspects of the application necessary to fulfill all its potential needs, we created a user interface flow chart to map out the app.

From this, a more developed prototype was created for user testing.

Deadman's Cellphone Poster and Animation

     Dead Man's Cellphone is a dark play about a woman who learns about a deadman through his cellphone. It explores how modern technology unites and isolates people.
     The particular rendition that this poster advertises is done in a more comical note. The style throws out the old and gruff red and black colors and opts for brighter and contrasting colors. Advertising to a college campus and the greater Atlanta area means bright colors and a modern feel. Dead Man's Cell Phone has an eclectic feel with dark twists and turns. It strays from realism as does the main graphic. The paradox of modern technology connecting yet isolating people is encompassed in the design.
