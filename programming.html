Colors of the Day

Colors of the Day

Colors of the Day explores a subset of pictures on flickr and creates a data visualization of them. This was coded using Java in Processing and use of the Flickr API.

A project done by many flickr users is to upload a picture everytime they see or do something that they determine. Sometimes a user will upload a picture of their view everytime they pass through a door. Other users might take a picture of every sign they see. There are even those that will take a picture of every squirrel they encounter. Done under the same hashtag, these slew of photos encompass everyday life.

I parsed through these photos and took in the data of when they were uploaded and the RGB value of each pixel in the photos. I split the data up into sections of the day and calculated which color (Red, Green, or Blue) each photo had the most of. They are displayed through bubbles on each screen of the data visualization. When hovered over, they display a random thumbnail photo from the grouping.

I found the data from the visualization interesting as there are many correlations that are made evident. Red dominated photos are prominant because yellow light and human fleshtones create photos with more red color. During the night time, there is more indoor photos and outside photos with outside lights. During the day there is a higher ratio of green and blue photos to red photos because there are more photos outside in the daylight with nature and blue skies.

Splash Application

Splash Application

Splash is an application I made with a group of peers. It sources data from the community to locate where clean drinkable water is. In America there are areas where clean water is not accessible yet access to technology and data is. This project was made in Android Studio and also made into a webapp.
